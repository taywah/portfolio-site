Comparable-Tool Churn Signal

A signal-discovery analysis: 16K target-market companies that recently stopped using a comparable platform, showed no detected replacement, and were not already covered by an active sales motion. The cohort validated 97% cold and sized to $960K-$3M annualized New MRR.

Role: Sole analyst — hypothesis, sizing, validation, write-up Stack: BigQuery, third-party technographics, HTML findings doc, Plotly quadrant chart Period: 2026 Q2

Problem & hypothesis

A third-party technographics source exposed enough historical usage metadata to test a simple hypothesis: companies that recently stopped using a comparable platform, with no replacement detected and no active sales coverage, may represent a quiet migration-intent signal.

Approach

flowchart TB
  Tech[Technographics history] --> Cohort[Companies with recent comparable-tool drop]
  Cohort --> Filter1[No replacement tool detected]
  Filter1 --> Filter2[Target-market fit]
  Filter2 --> Filter3[Coverage gate: no active sales motion]
  Filter3 --> Filter4[Customer gate: not already active]
  Filter4 --> Filter5[Activity gate: no recent sales activity]
  Filter5 --> Output[16K-company cold cohort]
  Output --> Sizing[$960K-$3M annualized New MRR sizing]
  Output --> Viz[Quadrant chart: recency x tenure x segment]

What I built

  • Cohort definition query: 16K target-market companies with a recently dropped comparable technology and no detected replacement in the same category.
  • 3-gate coverage scrub: removed companies that already had active opportunities, active customer status, or recent sales activity.
  • Sizing model: addressable market estimate times expected qualification, win-rate, and average contract-value assumptions, with a $960K-$3M annualized New MRR sensitivity band.
  • HTML findings document with a quadrant chart segmenting the cohort by:
    • X-axis: how recently the comparable tool disappeared
    • Y-axis: how long the company had used that tool
    • Color: product category or segment
    • Drill-down: target profile and industry

Validation

Worked with my manager to pressure-test the signal:

  • Added a tenure floor to filter out transient detection noise.
  • Validated the pattern on historical outcomes and confirmed the scrub produced a 97% cold-account cohort.
  • Checked for obvious seasonality and category-mix bias before recommending operational use.

Impact

  • Converted a noisy technographics field into a defensible, coverage-aware sales signal with 97% cold-account validation.
  • Created a sizing framework that separated the 16K-company headline audience from a realistically actionable $960K-$3M annualized New MRR opportunity.
  • Prepared the analysis for sales-team rollout before organizational priorities changed.
  • Documented a reusable methodology for future comparable-tool churn studies.

Lesson

The cheapest intent signal is often a comparable-tool churn signal, but only if you can prove the cohort is not already covered. The scrub is the rule-out, not the find. Without it, a large headline cohort can still be operationally useless.
