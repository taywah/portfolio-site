CRM Data Quality

Cross-functional CRM data-quality initiatives: 655 account websites cleaned, 6.5K accounts phone-type backfilled, and a reusable activity-attribution fix codified for future sales analyses.

Role: Discoverer, escalator, owner of the reusable rule Stack: BigQuery, CRM data, sales-engagement data, Cursor workspace rules Period: 2026 Q1-Q2

The Account-Enrichment Cleanup

Problem

Account enrichment depended on reliable website fields. Bad website data was corrupting downstream firmographic and industry classification because a key enrichment join used URL matching.

Approach

Cross-functional project with an operations partner. I built a cleanup workbook with account IDs, current websites, and proposed replacements. Operations validated the records and pushed approved updates through the CRM process.

Outcome

  • 655 accounts now have corrected websites driving downstream firmographic and industry matches.
  • 21 already-correct rows passed a QA back-check, confirming the process did not overwrite good records.
  • 1 stale account was flagged for follow-up cleanup.
  • Pattern reusable for future quarterly cleanups.

The Phone-Type Backfill

Operations needed a 6.5K-account launched cohort re-enriched with three phone-number types and formatted to a standard CRM upload template.

I scoped the enrichment, formatted the output, and coordinated with engineering on validation rules and update behavior so the upload could proceed without corrupting existing fields.

The Activity-Attribution Gap

Problem

While reviewing a reactivation contact list, I noticed several accounts showing "no recent contact" in analytical queries even though business partners had evidence of recent outreach.

Root cause

The root cause was a common CRM integration pattern: some third-party activities were attached at the contact level but not reliably populated at the account level. Account-only activity scrubs therefore undercounted recent outreach by roughly 20%-50% in affected workflows.

Diagnostic

flowchart LR
  Activity[Third-party sales activity] --> Sync[CRM sync]
  Sync --> Task["Activity row created"]
  Task --> ContactKey["Contact-level key populated"]
  Task --> AccountKey["Account-level key sometimes blank"]
  ContactKey --> ContactLookup["Contact-to-account lookup workaround"]
  AccountKey --> Bug[Account-only queries miss activity]

Resolution path

  1. Reproduced the issue with 10 anonymized examples that showed recent activity present at contact grain but missing from account-grain queries.
  2. Escalated the issue with a clear explanation of why it affected downstream sales analytics.
  3. Routed the upstream integration fix through the appropriate operations owner.
  4. In parallel, codified the workaround as a reusable workspace rule so future activity scrubs use both direct account keys and contact-to-account rollups.

The Codified Workspace Rule

The rule documents both the safe query pattern and the risky account-only pattern side-by-side, with the rationale baked in. Simplified excerpt:

## Safer Pattern

recently_contacted_accounts AS (
   SELECT account_key
   FROM activity_table
   WHERE account_key IS PRESENT
   UNION DISTINCT
   SELECT contact_to_account.account_key
   FROM activity_table
   JOIN contact_to_account
     ON activity_table.contact_key = contact_to_account.contact_key
   WHERE account_key IS MISSING
)

## Risky Pattern

recently_contacted_accounts AS (
   SELECT account_key
   FROM activity_table
)

Impact

  • Corrected 655 account-enrichment inputs so downstream firmographic and industry matches were more reliable.
  • Backfilled phone-type coverage for 6.5K accounts in a format operations could upload safely.
  • Submitted the upstream integration issue while shipping a local analytical workaround immediately.
  • Pattern documented at the workspace level so future analysts do not re-introduce the gap.

Lesson

Data-quality escalation works better when you ship the workaround and the upstream ticket in parallel. Do the right thing locally, then codify the pattern so every future query benefits even before the platform fix lands.
