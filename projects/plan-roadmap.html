PlanRoadmap — Sales Play Sizing Pipeline

A CSV-in/CSV-out pipeline that takes 53 leadership-defined sales plays and computes addressable market, reachable audience, expected qualification, revenue assumptions, win-rate signal, and opportunity size for each.

Role: Sole designer / builder / debugger Stack: Python, BigQuery, pandas, local cache, warehouse-backed lookup tables Period: 2026 Q2

Problem

Sales leadership defined 53 plays across intent, technology, and vertical themes. Each play needed quantified sizing across addressable market, reachable audience, revenue assumptions, win-rate signal, and opportunity size. Manual sizing would not scale.

The input data was messy: some plays had explicit filter columns, while others only had descriptive headlines that needed to be parsed into structured filters.

Approach

Five-way play classifier; warehouse-backed technology lookup; fallback logic for low-signal plays; and outlier-deal alerts when historical revenue assumptions were likely distorted by unusual wins.

flowchart TB
  CSV[Input CSV: 53 sales plays] --> Classifier[5-way classifier]
  Classifier --> Intent[Intent topic mapping]
  Classifier --> Tech[Technology lookup]
  Classifier --> Industry[NAICS prefix from industry name]
  Classifier --> Combo[Industry + Tech combined filter]
  Classifier --> Headline[Headline parser for ICP x Tech plays]
  Intent --> Sizing
  Tech --> Sizing
  Industry --> Sizing
  Combo --> Sizing
  Headline --> Sizing[Market size, reachable audience, revenue assumptions, win rate, opportunity size]
  Sizing --> Output[Output CSV with audit notes]

What I built

  • 5-way play classifier: Intent / Tech / Industry / Industry+Tech / Headline-parsed ICP×Tech
  • Warehouse-backed technology lookup across 32K tracked technologies, replacing a brittle 200-row hand-curated mapping file.
  • Wildcard parent-brand expansion so broad product families mapped to all relevant tracked variants instead of a single narrow match; one broad-product play moved from 398 to 192,904 addressable companies after expansion.
  • Fallback logic: low-signal plays fall back to motion-level averages so sparse history does not create false precision: $523.71 average AOV, $382.50 median AOV, 24.06% win rate, and 38.32% SOM/SAM ratio.
  • Outlier-deal alert appended to the notes column when a play's historical revenue assumptions were likely distorted by unusually large wins.
  • Referenced-lookup notes in the output for reviewer auditability.
  • Intent topic resolution layer maps business-language play names to governed warehouse topics.
  • Debugging summary documenting 19 silent-failure patterns and fixes as a teaching artifact for the next analyst.

Silent-failure bug exemplars

  1. Date-key type mismatch: two source tables represented fiscal periods differently, causing silent zero-match joins until the keys were normalized.
  2. Parent-brand under-expansion: a narrow manual mapping missed related product variants until the lookup layer supported wildcard expansion.
  3. Missing-value fallthrough: spreadsheet blanks became truthy placeholder values in pandas, blocking fallback logic until explicit null checks were added.

Impact

  • Sized 50 of 53 leadership-defined plays end-to-end; the 3 remaining gaps reflected missing upstream signal rather than pipeline failure.
  • Batch runtime reached roughly 5 minutes warm and 12 minutes cold for all 53 plays, fast enough for repeated planning cycles rather than one-off analysis.
  • Live-computed fallback assumptions became reusable across other sizing work: $523.71 average AOV, 24.06% win rate, and 38.32% SOM/SAM ratio.
  • The debugging summary became the guide for extending the sizing pattern safely.

What I'd do differently

I shipped the reachable-audience estimate as a ratio-based approximation for the proof of concept, which was defensible for speed but became the bottleneck for a final version. In retrospect I would have started the full persona-scrub infrastructure in parallel.
